I'm a metadata archivist in the
Manuscripts and Archives Department
at Yale University.


In my job I use a variety of methods
to analyze, create and enhance archival metadata in bulk.
I also create tools and develop procedures
to make archival work more efficient.
And I still, sometimes, process collections.


I am particularly interested in how archivists can
leverage powerful scripting languages and
other technologies to make the most of our
increasingly limited time time and resources.


This is my website, where I keep
some of my professional work,
especially my coding projects,
writing, and presentations.
